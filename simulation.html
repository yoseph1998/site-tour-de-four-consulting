Introduction

Tour De Four was given the task to complete an analysis to produce 12’’ balance bikes. For this section, Neil Eno was tasked to fix a problem that would arise from Tour De Four’s facility. The question he answered was which production would prove more beneficial to the company as they were to produce the balance bikes. The two layouts to be compared are Product line layout and Process Layout. Neil used two simulation to prove which one would be the better of the two.

Production Line vs. Process

Before getting into all the simulations it would help to understand what the two layouts are and what makes them different. Production Line layout. Is a linear process where after the material is completed in one process it then moved to the next. Production is known for mass production of products. Process Layout are for similar process are combined and moves from one group to the next. Process Layout is also good for moderate production in facilities. Though the layouts do not seem similar it is still a question of which to choose for the companies’ facility.

Simulation

In the production line, there are four processes: Press/Cutting Process where the material is cut and shaped, Welding Process where all the parts are welded together, Paint Process where all of the metal materials are painted, and Assembly where all the non-metal materials are put onto the bike frame. After it goes through the processes, it is then sent to a combiner where the bike and source box is combined into the final product. The way the material is moved from one process to the next is conveyors. After all the numbers were put into each process the production layout produced 1048 bikes during one work shift.
