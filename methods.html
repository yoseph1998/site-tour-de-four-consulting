Methods Analysis

The methods analysis performed by Tour De Four Consulting (TDFC) resulted in the following recommendations for years 1-5 for Marathon Investments. Calculations focus on the year five volume of 237,000 units to be produced. TDFC recognized 65 work elements to complete the partial assembly and finished product packaging of a balance bike. TDFC has chosen to run one shift from 8:00am to 4:30pm which totals to 8.5 hours. This shift consists of 7.5 hours of production, two paid 15 min breaks, and an unpaid lunch resulting in a total of 8 hrs paid per day per employee.

Takt time

Our Takt time was found to be 0.48min or 28.8 seconds. We simply divided our year five expected sale of 237,000 units by the total production minutes per year of 114,750 units. Total production minutes was found by multiplying the total number of production weeks x five production days per week x hours per day x mins per hour.

Workstations

The number of workstations to have the line balanced mathematically at 85% efficiency came in at 10. However, running 10 workstations left certain workstations exceeding the takt time and working at 100%. So, an 11th workstation was added to help minimize the load. Even at 11 workstations we had two workstations exceeding 90% efficiency. At 12 workstations we reduced all workstations to 90% and below with an overall efficiency of 71%.

Labor Cost of assembly

Labor cost was calculated using the average assembly worker salary in the area of Olive Branch divided by our production hours per yer. Which is $25,895.00 / 2040 hrs = $12.69 per hour per employee.

Assembly area labor cost = (hour rate) * (# of employees) * (yearly paid work hours)

Assembly area yearly labor cost = 12.69 * 12 * 2040 = $308,937.60 a year
